Feijoada (Brazilian Black Bean Stew)

Ingredients

  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 ½ cups chopped onion, divided
  • ½ cup green onions, chopped
  • 1 clove garlic, chopped
  • 1 (12 ounce) package dry black beans, soaked overnight
  • 2 smoked ham hocks
  • 8 ounces diced ham
  • ½ pound thickly sliced bacon, diced
  • 2 bay leaves, crushed
  • ⅛ teaspoon ground coriander
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • ½ cup chopped fresh cilantro (Optional)
  • ¼ cup chopped fresh parsley (Optional)

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Ingredients to make Brazilian black bean stew Dotdash Meredith Food Studios
  3. Heat oil in a large pot or Dutch oven. Add 3/4 cup chopped onion, green onions, and garlic; cook and stir until softened, about 4 minutes.
  4. A large pot with chopped onions and green onions Dotdash Meredith Food Studios
  5. Pour in soaked beans and fill with enough water to cover beans by 3 inches. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to medium-low, and simmer uncovered for 2 hours, or until tender.
  6. A large pot with soaked beans, water, and chopped onion Dotdash Meredith Food Studios
  7. While beans are cooking, place ham hocks in a smaller pot with 1/4 cup chopped onion.
  8. A small pot with two ham hocks and chopped onion Dotdash Meredith Food Studios
  9. Cover with water and simmer until meat pulls off of the bone easily, about 1 hour.
  10. A cutting board with boiled ham hocks with a fork removing meat Dotdash Meredith Food Studios
  11. Drain and add to beans. Meanwhile, preheat the oven to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
  12. A pot of black bean stew with pieces of ham hock Dotdash Meredith Food Studios
  13. Place ham, bacon, and remaining onion in a baking dish. Bake until mixture is crispy, 15 minutes.
  14. A baking dish with chopped ham, bacon, and onion Dotdash Meredith Food Studios
  15. Drain bacon and ham mixture and add to beans. Season with bay leaves, coriander, salt, and pepper. Simmer, uncovered, 30 minutes more.
  16. A pot of seasoned bean stew with bacon, ham, and bay leaves Dotdash Meredith Food Studios
  17. Stir in chopped cilantro and parsley just before serving.
